# Demo Video Package

Updated: 2026-07-14

## Recording gate

- The previously generated English cut is a stale historical candidate. It does not represent the current 11v11 theatre, fan rooms, or the new continuous-narration standard.
- Do not record, render, publish, or replace the public demo until the current product passes local acceptance, the same Git commit passes online review, the final script is delivered, and the project owner explicitly approves recording.
- Voice samples, profile identifiers, local voice paths, tokens, and JWTs remain outside the repository and public media.

The target remains one English judging cut under five minutes, rendered at 1280x720 with H.264 video and AAC stereo narration after approval.

## Required judging story

The final cut must visibly prove all three official requirements:

1. The fan problem: fragmented match context, missed moments, weak low-friction participation, and confusing source trust.
2. The live product walkthrough: Match Center, 11v11 Pulse Play, score challenge, AI catch-up, fan rooms, replay, teams, languages, mobile use, and official viewing links.
3. How TxLINE powers the backend: authenticated fixture, score/event, and official-odds collections; normalization; freshness; provisional reversal; and `game_finalised` settlement without exposing credentials.

One English cut keeps the submission unambiguous and maps the working product to fan accessibility, real-time responsiveness, originality, commercial path, and completeness.

## Narration standard

- Record a small number of continuous chapters rather than many isolated short sentences.
- Use natural bridge phrases between the problem, product, TxLINE, commercial path, and proof sections.
- Keep match moments energetic, safety and source boundaries calm, and the close confident.
- Avoid clipped sentence starts, repeated pauses, abrupt loudness changes, and mechanical per-scene cadence.
- After rendering, watch and listen to the entire program twice from start to finish. A collection of passing scene checks is not a substitute for two complete reviews.

## Reproducible generation after approval

The scene manifest lives in `scripts/record-demo-video.mjs`. The current screenshots are generated by the final browser E2E and copied to the ignored `demo-assets/screenshots/` directory.

```powershell
python scripts\render-demo-video.py --variant B
& .\scripts\render-demo-narration.ps1 -Variant B
```

`npm run demo:video` performs the same two steps, but must not be run before approval. The renderer uses the local FFmpeg encoder. When `WCLP_VOICE_PROFILE_ID` is set locally, the authorized local voice service receives English emotion direction; the profile ID is never written into source, output captions, or the public bundle.

## Acceptance after recording

- Two complete watch/listen passes from first frame to final frame.
- Full decode with zero corruption or missing-picture errors.
- Reverse transcription checked against the approved English script.
- Duration, silence, loudness, clipping, pronunciation, and audio/video sync checked.
- Problem, product walkthrough, TxLINE boundary, community, commercial path, evidence, and safety frames inspected.
- No `.env.local`, token, JWT, wallet, browser storage, personal account content, voice profile, or local path appears.
